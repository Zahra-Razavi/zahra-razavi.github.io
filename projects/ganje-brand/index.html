A brand language designed for scale

The Ganje identity had to function across multiple contexts: tiny IoT screens, courier tablets, mobile apps, and large urban billboards. We engineered a modular system that keeps the brand instantly recognizable, even when the UI is "faceless" and the experience shifts to the user's phone.

From naming and archetypes through typography, iconography, and UI components, the system flexes from operations dashboards to customer interactions without sacrificing clarity or warmth.

Logo

Dual-state marks align with customer environments: bright locker bays and low-light courier hubs. The wordmark maintains generous tracking for legibility on hardware, while the monogram sustains recognition in app icons.

Anchors

  • Rounded geometry references locker bays and NFC halo interactions.
  • Extended tracking keeps the wordmark legible at 12px and 12ft alike.

Usage

  • Monogram anchors app icons and NFC wayfinding.
  • Full wordmark owns sales decks, lockers, and billboards.

Typography

UI · Web & Mobile

Typeface

Inter

  • Google font

    • Usage notes

    • Maintain 16px minimum body size for accessibility.
    • Scale headings responsively on mobile breakpoints.
    • Leverage Inter’s optical optimization for crisp UI typography.

    Hierarchy

    • Heading 1:
    • 32px (Desktop) / 24px (Mobile) · Bold · Line-height 1.2
    • Heading 2:
    • 24px / 20px · Medium · Line-height 1.3
    • Body:
    • 16px / 14px · Regular · Line-height 1.5–1.6
    • Caption:
    • 14px / 12px · Light · Uppercase · Letter-spacing +0.05em

    Print systems

    Typeface

    Inter

  • Desktop published

    • Usage notes

    • Increase letter-spacing slightly for print clarity.
    • Keep backgrounds clean and maintain high contrast.
    • Apply brand colors to section titles for emphasis.

    Hierarchy

    • Titles (H1):
      • 48pt · Bold
    • Subtitles (H2):
    • 36pt · Medium
    • Body:
    • 11–12pt · Regular
    • Captions:
    • 9pt · Light · Uppercase

    UI · Web & Mobile

    Typeface

    IRANYekan

    Usage notes

    • Line-height slightly larger than English (Persian script needs breathing space).
    • Ensure RTL alignment in web/mobile layouts.
    • Don’t mix Inter + IRANYekan in the same line; switch by language context.

    Hierarchy

    • Heading 1:
    • 32px (Desktop) / 24px (Mobile) · Bold · Line-height 1.3
    • Heading 2:
    • 24px / 20px · Medium · Line-height 1.6
    • Body:
    • 16px / 14px · Regular · Line-height 1.6
    • Caption:
    • 14px / 12px · Light · with subtle letter-spacing

    Print systems

    Typeface

    IRANYekan

    Usage notes

    • Use ample line spacing to prevent script overlap.
    • Maintain high ink density (avoid light weights in print).
    • Use brand palette sparingly — Persian typography benefits from high contrast black/white for clarity.

    Hierarchy

    • Titles (H1):
      • 48pt · Bold
    • Subtitles (H2):
    • 36pt · Medium
    • Body:
    • 11–12pt · Regular
    • Captions:
    • 9pt · Light · Uppercase
