Case Study . Product Design

Notify Me! A Shopify App

Building a cohesive customization experience for multi-channel notifications, aligning the Shopify Polaris design language with merchant workflows and customer expectations.

Role
Solo product designer
Timeline
Jan 2022 – Jun 2022
Scope
User research, UX design, UI design
Tools & resources
Figma, Hotjar, Lucky Orange, Intercom, Jira, Mailchimp, Polaris

Tools & Resources

Problem

Merchants adopted Notify Me! to keep shoppers in the loop when high-demand items restocked or when pre-orders shipped. As channels expanded beyond email, the customization experience lagged behind, generating an influx of support tickets and fragmented brand touchpoints.

Users could not customize Push or SMS notifications for critical categories such as reminder notifications and pre-order confirmations. Only email templates were editable, creating a brand inconsistency and a high volume of customer support tickets.

Responding to merchant feedback, Push notifications were introduced. However, the release lacked flexible configuration for push widgets and message styling, leaving users without the ability to adapt the experience to their storefront and audience tone.

Discovery

A Polaris-aligned discovery process combined qualitative depth with quantitative guardrails to reveal how merchants shaped notification flows and where they struggled.

Qualitative

User behaviour analysis

20 merchant session recordings triangulated to uncover friction in multi-channel setup.

Heatmaps & click tracking

Navigation hotspots surfaced the need for clearer channel labeling.

Chat support feedback

Intercom transcripts mapped support tickets to missing customization controls.

Quantitative

Churn rate

Tracked post-launch churn trends to ensure personalization reduced attrition.

Onboarding churn rate

Measured drop-off between channel selection and template activation.

Notification user flow

The redesigned flow grounded each step in Polaris patterns—surface hierarchy, resource lists, and modal summaries—to guide merchants from channel selection to launch-ready notifications without context switching.

  1. 1. Channel overview

    Polaris card layout highlights readiness status for email, SMS, and push.

  2. 2. Template customization

    Inline editor supports copy, media, and branding adjustments with live previews.

  3. 3. Trigger rules

    Event settings leverage Polaris resource lists for quick association.

  4. 4. Launch confirmation

    Summary sheet confirms cadence, audience, and fallback notifications.

Email setup user flow

The email flow modernized legacy templates with modular Polaris components, creating consistent structures for pre-order confirmations, restock alerts, and reminders.

  1. 1. Template selection

    Scenario-based templates help merchants pick the right communication moment.

  2. 2. Content structure

    Drag-and-drop Polaris sections enable quick experimentation with layout hierarchy.

  3. 3. Personalization tokens

    Dynamic fields surface shopper data, ensuring messaging stays personal.

  4. 4. Delivery cadence

    Scheduler aligns send frequency with inventory availability and shopper preferences.

Flow visuals

High-fidelity flows combine Polaris components with branded surfaces, ready for stakeholder reviews and engineering handoff.
