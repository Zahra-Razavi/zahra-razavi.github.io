Problem

SME lending is often messy — slow approvals, scattered communication, and endless document exchanges between brokers and lenders. Deals get lost in emails, and compliance steps create bottlenecks.

Lendeck set out to fix that by building one clear, connected platform that brings structure, speed, and transparency to the lending process.

Our Solution

Lendeck is a unified dashboard that connects brokers, lenders, and merchants in one intuitive space.

It automates data collection, simplifies deal submission, and ensures compliance from day one — cutting approval times in half and replacing back-and-forth emails with smart, transparent workflows.

Discovery

To fully understand the ecosystem, I conducted research and interviews with all three user types to uncover workflow challenges and pain points in the lending process.

Actions

  • Mapped the end-to-end lending process to identify inefficiencies.
  • Defined user roles and responsibilities across brokers, lenders, and merchants.
  • Validated assumptions through interviews and testing.

FinTech and Lending Background Research

I analyzed the lending industry’s digital transformation, focusing on trends in automation, open banking APIs, and AI-based risk evaluation. This helped shape the product vision toward a smarter, compliance-first lending experience.

Interview Stakeholders

Stakeholders (product, legal, risk, and tech teams) shared insights about operational bottlenecks, compliance rules, and strategic goals for automation and transparency.

Interview Users of All 3 Sides

  • Brokers struggled with fragmented document workflows and slow lender responses.
  • Lenders faced inconsistent data quality and manual risk assessments.
  • Merchants lacked visibility into their loan status and document requirements.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed key competitors — BanadaExchange.com, CashAdvance.com, and other potential B2B lending tools — to benchmark usability, feature sets, and communication flows. This informed our differentiation strategy focusing on automation, transparency, and human-centered design.

Insights

Users desired transparency, automation, and trust. Speed of decision-making and clarity of communication were crucial. The insight led to designing a dashboard that visualizes deal progress, centralizes communication, and automates document handling.

Journey map

The journey visualized handoffs across brokers, lenders, and merchants, surfacing where automation and guidance could reduce friction.

Site Map

User Flow

Each user type’s flow was optimized to reduce steps and cognitive load. Brokers can submit deals in under three minutes; lenders review with automated insights; merchants upload documents with guided prompts.

Prototype & Test

Low-fidelity prototypes were created in Figma and tested with eight participants across roles. Usability tests after each iteration ensured smoother navigation and reduced confusion in document management.

Visual Design

The interface combines a modern, trustworthy aesthetic with FinTech clarity—using a clean layout, contrast-driven hierarchy, and contextual microinteractions. I applied atomic design principles to maintain consistency across modules and scalable components.
