Case Study · Product Design

Kidora — Cognitive Growth App for Kids

Kidora is a child healthcare mobile application designed to empower parents by offering personalized, science-based developmental programs. It provides age-appropriate and comprehensive cognitive activities that support every stage of a child’s growth.

Key Features:

  • Personalized games to nurture the child’s cognitive development
  • Standardized scientific tests to evaluate developmental progress
  • Tools to record and track key milestones in the child’s growth
  • Expert information to help parents raise healthy children and unlock their cognitive potential

Role

Digital Product Designer

Timeline

Sep 2020 – Dec 2022

Main Contributions

  • User research to deeply understand parents’ behaviors, expectations, and stressors.
  • Iterative flow, navigation, and feature design aligned with cognitive development milestones.
  • Data-driven UX analysis enhanced by biofeedback and eye-tracking insights.

Process & Methods

I followed the Design Thinking process—empathizing with parents and experts, defining the problem space, ideating solutions, prototyping rapidly, and testing repeatedly. Each iteration focused on translating research into confident caregiving moments.

Empathy & Define

I combined primary and desk research with interviews to access deep stories about caregiving routines. Insights about cultural expectations and time constraints shaped how features surfaced at each step.

Challenges

  • Diverse user needs with limited behavioral benchmarks demanded extensive qualitative research.
  • Lack of Persian market references required translating global best practices to local contexts.
  • Design guidelines for multilingual experiences were scarce, complicating interface rules.
  • Mobile eye-tracking data lacked fidelity, so web-based testing supplemented findings.

Personas

Ten colors are considered as the main colors that are used in the main pages of the mobile application as the colors of each category. In fact, each of these colors represents one of the areas of cognitive development based on the ASQ test. Ten main colors have been selected from the entire natural color spectrum to enable the differentiation of all categories. 4 shads of each primary color are used to display the elements of the user interface of the product, such as the background, text color.

Prototyping & Testing

I validated every design decision through an iterative cycle of prototyping and user testing. Using Figma, I built both low- and high-fidelity prototypes and tested each feature shortly after design completion to ensure usability and clarity. Each testing round engaged 5–8 parents and caregivers, whose feedback guided rapid refinements and helped shape a more intuitive and engaging user experience.

Using User Testing and Usability testing I observed the user behavior and identified where they tend to bounce off the website. This helped to identify the pages that had the hardest experience for the users. I conducted Eye-tracking Test on the pages identified and suggested a redesign for such pages.

Quantitative task success metrics and System Usability Scale snapshots tracked improvements, guiding roadmap priorities for onboarding, assessments, and report sharing.

Deliverables

Shared Figma files packaged component specs, motion timings, and tokenized styles so development could lift assets directly without breaking the crafted interactions.
