Case Study · Product Design

Kidora, Cognitive Growth App for Kids

Kidora is a child healthcare mobile application designed to empower parents by offering personalized, science-based developmental programs. It provides age-appropriate and comprehensive cognitive activities that support every stage of a child’s growth.

Key Features:

  • Personalized games to nurture the child’s cognitive development
  • Standardized scientific tests to evaluate developmental progress
  • Tools to record and track key milestones in the child’s growth
  • Expert information to help parents raise healthy children and unlock their cognitive potential

Role

Digital Product Designer

Timeline

Sep 2020 – Dec 2022

Main Contributions

  • User research to deeply understand parents’ behaviors, expectations, and stressors.
  • Iterative flow, navigation, and feature design aligned with cognitive development milestones.
  • Data-driven UX analysis enhanced by biofeedback and eye-tracking insights.

Challenges

  • Diverse user needs with limited behavioral benchmarks demanded extensive qualitative research.
  • Lack of Persian market references required translating global best practices to local contexts.
  • Design guidelines for multilingual experiences were scarce, complicating interface rules.
  • Mobile eye-tracking data lacked fidelity, so web-based testing supplemented findings.

Process

I followed the Design Thinking process—empathizing with parents and experts, defining the problem space, ideating solutions, prototyping rapidly, and testing repeatedly. Each iteration focused on translating research into confident caregiving moments.

Persona

15 user interviews revealed patterns that led to three key personas representing Kidora’s core audience. These profiles captured parents’ needs, motivations, and challenges, guiding a more empathetic and purposeful product experience.

Site Map

Through wireframing and idea visualization, we structured Kidora’s user journeys into a clear site map. Insights from earlier research shaped the information architecture, ensuring intuitive navigation and logical flow between features.

Color Palette

Kidora’s visual system includes ten hero colors, each symbolizing a specific area of cognitive development and childcare activities. Every color set features accessible shades and gradients, giving the interface a friendly, educational, and cohesive look.

Prototyping & Testing

Each design decision was validated through continuous prototyping and user testing in Figma. Both low- and high-fidelity prototypes were tested with 5–8 parents and caregivers per round to refine usability and engagement.

Through usability and eye-tracking tests, I identified friction points and improved key user flows. Quantitative metrics and SUS scores guided iterative refinements across onboarding, assessments, and report-sharing experiences.
